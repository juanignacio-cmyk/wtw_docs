Accessible Tourism Market (Dataintelo 2026)

  • Market 2024: $92.7 Bn
  • Projected 2033: $172.8 Bn (CAGR 7.1%)
  • Asia Pacific = fastest growing region
  • Verification segment: Third-Party Verified, Self-Declared, Standards-Compliant, Not Rated
  • WTW listed as key player in competitive landscape (#1 in company profiles)
  • Key driver: disability rights enforcement, airport/rail upgrades, destination certification programs
  • Opportunity: platforms combining granular photo-verified accessibility + dynamic packaging

State of Destination Marketing 2026 (Sojern)

  • 359 respondents, Sep-Oct 2025
  • 82% of North American DMOs operate local/municipal/regional level
  • Top strategic influences: consumer behavior (80%), destination developments (65%), economic volatility (59%)
  • 41% increased digital ad budgets in 2026 (down from 44% in 2025)
  • 75% NA DMOs say budgets stable 1-2 years; only 56% Europe
  • Economic impact (#1 metric) — 72% say conversion/ROI and economic impact are top stakeholder priorities
  • 79% NA DMOs say economic impact = #1 stakeholder metric
  • Budget challenges: 61% say budget is top hurdle (up from 51%)
  • Social media advertising: 88% of DMOs invest (Instagram 97%, Facebook 90%)
  • Always-on campaigns: 65% (up from 51% in 2025)
  • Personalization: 45% "moderate", 22% "basic", only 9% "advanced"
  • Top KPIs: CTR, CPC, hotel bookings (emerging), website engagement, ROAS
  • Key challenge: 49% say tracking/attribution is top barrier to full-funnel
  • 52% say political/policy factors have moderate-significant impact on strategy
  • DMO team sizes: 1/3 have 10 or fewer FTEs
  • Funding: NA = tourism/hotel tax (more stable); Europe = public funding (more volatile)

Key Insights for WTW Sales

  • DMOs under pressure to prove ECONOMIC IMPACT (not vanity metrics) → WTW's Economic Impact Dashboard is exactly what they need
  • Budget constraints are real → WTW pricing must show clear ROI
  • Always-on is trending → opportunity for recurring AV subscriptions

  • Attribution is the #1 pain → WTW's booking data could help solve this
