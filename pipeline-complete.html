The Problem

❌ Lifecycle = Pipeline

SQL, MQL, and Prospecting are lifecycle stages being used as deal stages. Contacts and deals are mixed. This inflates the pipeline and breaks forecasting.

❌ No Discovery Stage

Team jumps from "interested" to "demo" without understanding the customer's problem. They try to "generate the problem" during the sale.

❌ Enrichment in Pipeline

SDR work (enrichment, outreach) is tracked as deal stages. This inflates pipeline with deals that aren't real opportunities.

❌ No Stage Gates

No required fields per stage. Anyone can move deals anywhere. No accountability, no data quality.

02 The New Model

Core principle (Gap Selling): The deal pipeline begins at Discovery — not before. Everything before Discovery is SDR workflow, not sales pipeline.

Three Layers, Clearly Separated

👤 Lifecycle Stage

Where is the CONTACT in their journey?

Subscriber → Lead → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Customer → Evangelist

Lives on the Contact/Company. Updated automatically.

📋 Lead Status

What is the SDR doing with this contact?

New → Enriching → Outreach → Meeting Booked → Meeting Held

Lives on the Contact. SDR's workspace.

💰 Deal Pipeline

Where is this DEAL in the buying process?

Discovery → Demo → Proposal → Negotiation → Closing Soon → Won/Lost

Lives on the Deal. Starts when meeting is booked.

How They Connect

SDR WORKFLOW (pre-deal)
Contact created → Lead Status: New → Enriching → Outreach → Meeting Booked
↓ DEAL CREATED (Discovery Scheduled) ↓
DEAL PIPELINE (sales)
Discovery → Demo → Proposal Sent → Negotiation → Closing Soon → Won / Lost
LIFECYCLE SYNCS AUTOMATICALLY
Contact created → Lead · Intent signal → MQL · Discovery scheduled (deal created) → SQL · Gap confirmed (moves to Demo) → Opportunity · Won → Customer · Refers/case study → Evangelist
Why this matters: Enrichment and Outreach are NOT deal stages. They're SDR activities tracked via Lead Status. This keeps the pipeline clean — only real opportunities with scheduled meetings appear as deals.

🔎 Discovery — Diagnose the GapPipeline
Owner

Key Metric

Conversion Target
Discovery → Demo: 70%
Deal creation: SDR books meeting → creates deal → AE receives with full enrichment context. AE prepares for the call using company data, outreach history, and ICP tier.
Required at deal creation: Contact, Company, B2B Source, ICP Tier, Deal Type
Required after meeting: Gap Summary, Champion Type, A11Y Maturity (validated)
What happens: 30-45 min call. AE diagnoses the gap using Gap Selling framework:
  • Current State: Where are they today? What's happening (or not) with accessibility?
  • Impact: What's the cost of doing nothing? (lost travelers, compliance risk, reputation)
  • Root Cause: Why hasn't this been solved? (no data, no budget, no expertise)
  • Future State: What would success look like for them?
  • The Gap: The distance between current and future state = the sale
Exit (Success): Gap confirmed + next step agreed → Demo (3-4 days later)
Exit (No gap): → Closed Lost (reason: "No problem identified")
Example: "Visit Nashville has 150+ hotels, zero verified. They lost a $500K group booking last year because a wheelchair user couldn't find accessible options. Their board is asking about ADA compliance but they have no data. Gap = massive. Demo scheduled for Thursday."

11 Gap Selling Framework

Core principle: "Nobody buys anything without having a problem first." — Keenan. Your job is to diagnose the gap between current state and future state. The bigger the gap, the more urgent the sale.
😰
Current State
Where they are today
Problems, inefficiencies, risks
← THE GAP →
This is what you're selling
🎯
Future State
Where they want to be
Goals, outcomes, impact

Key Principles

🚫 Don't lead with BANT

Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline should emerge from Discovery, not be a checklist the SDR fills before the meeting. Keenan: "BANT is bunk."

🔍 Discovery = The Close

A well-executed Discovery is where the sale is won. A demo without diagnosis is generic and lowers win rate.

🎯 Sell the Gap, Not Features

Nobody cares about your product. They care about closing the gap between their current pain and their desired outcome.

💡 The Buyer Doesn't Know Their Problem

This is why Discovery ≠ Demo. In Discovery, you help them SEE the problem. In Demo, you show how you SOLVE it.

🎯 Action Plan

Principle: No data gets deleted or disrupted. We rename, add, and mirror — then clean.

Steps (in order)

#ActionWhat ChangesImpact on Team
1 Rename pipeline stages
Match stages to new model (Discovery → Demo → Proposal → Negotiation → Closing Soon → Won/Lost)		 Stage names change. Deals stay where they are. Zero — just labels
2 Add Discovery stage
Insert as first stage in the deal pipeline		 New stage available. No deals forced into it. Zero — opt-in for new deals
3 Mirror deals → Lifecycle Stage
For every contact with an active deal today, set their Lifecycle Stage based on deal status (e.g., has active deal = Opportunity, has won deal = Customer)		 Contacts get a lifecycle stage that reflects reality. Deals untouched. Zero — automated/bulk
4 Close dead deals as Closed Lost
Now that lifecycle is separate, closing a deal does NOT change the contact/company stage. A lost deal ≠ a lost relationship.		 Pipeline shrinks to real opportunities only. Contact lifecycle stays intact. Pipeline looks smaller — but it's TRUE
5 New process for new contacts
Contact → Lead Status workflow → Deal only when Discovery is booked		 No more Contact = Deal from day 1 SDR shift — fewer deals, higher quality

🔑 Why this order matters

Step 3 is the key: once contacts have their own lifecycle stage independent of deals, you can safely close dead deals (Step 4) without losing the relationship. Today, closing a deal feels like "killing" the contact — because they're the same thing. After the mirror, they're decoupled.

19 Frequently Asked Questions

When is a deal created?

When a Discovery meeting is booked. Not before. The deal represents an active sales opportunity, not a contact we're prospecting.

Can one contact have multiple deals?

Yes. If Visit Phoenix was Closed Lost last year but you reconnected at the Summit → same contact, new deal. Old deal stays as history.

What's the difference between Lifecycle and Pipeline?

Lifecycle = where the CONTACT is (Lead, MQL, SQL, Customer). Pipeline = where the DEAL is (Discovery, Demo, Proposal). They sync automatically but are NOT the same thing.

Where does the SDR track their work?

In Lead Status (contact property): New → Enriching → Outreach → Meeting Booked. NOT in the deal pipeline. SDR work is pre-deal.

Who should do outreach — SDR or AE?

SDR for cold contacts (event tarjetas, Apollo, outbound). AE keeps warm contacts where they had a real conversation. Rule: if there's a personal relationship → AE maintains it.

Why no SQL stage in the pipeline?

SQL is a lifecycle stage, not a deal stage. Per Gap Selling, BANT should emerge from Discovery, not be a pre-meeting checklist. SQL = "meeting booked" (lifecycle) not a pipeline stage deals sit in.

What about "Circle Back"?

Use Closed Lost with reason "Not now — timing" + a reactivation date. A workflow auto-creates a task when the date arrives. Max 2 reactivations. This prevents zombie deals.

What if the AE discovers there's no real problem in Discovery?

→ Closed Lost (reason: "No problem identified"). This is normal and healthy. It means the pipeline only has real opportunities.

How do we track what generates pipeline?

Every deal has a B2B Source property (Outbound, Inbound, Referral, Trade Show). This tells you which sources generate the most Discovery meetings and wins.

Discovery and Demo on the same call?

No. They are two meetings with different objectives. Discovery = understand the problem. Demo = present the solution. Doing both at once leads to generic demos that don't address the real pain. Gap between them: 3-4 days.
