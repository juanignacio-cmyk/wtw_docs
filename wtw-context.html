Wheel the World - Context

What they do

  • Building the accessibility data layer for travel/tourism industry
  • Verify & structure accessibility data across 200+ standardized data points per site
  • Cover: hotels, attractions, restaurants, cruise lines, airports, convention centers
  • HQ: 149 New Montgomery, 4th Floor, San Francisco CA 94105

Core Product: Accessibility Verified

  • Annual program for destinations & hospitality businesses
  • On-ground verification by trained "Mappers" using Accessibility Mapping System App
  • Data becomes bookable inventory on wheeltheworld.com + distributed via 3rd party channels (OTAs, metasearch, AI tools, hotel websites, destination pages)

Value Prop (3 pillars)

  • Market — Increase visitation from travelers w/ disabilities & seniors. SEO landing pages, community, OTA, Destination Hubs, AI trip planning
  • Verify — Standardized on-site assessments, dashboards, benchmarks, scores, improvement roadmaps
  • Improve — Ongoing support, workshops, WTW Academy, enablement toolkits

Market Size

  • US: 13M travelers w/ disabilities, 38M+ trips/year, $25B spend on accommodations/tours/activities
  • 74% encounter accessibility obstacles (info gap, not infrastructure)
  • ~$19B wasted annually due to info gap
  • Global opportunity: $120B accessible travel market (growing w/ aging population)

Target Customers

  • DMOs (Destination Marketing Organizations)
  • Hotels, restaurants, hospitality businesses
  • Selling points: incremental demand, reduced risk (refunds/lawsuits), brand equity, marketing ROI

Key Insight

The problem isn't infrastructure — it's DATA. Accessibility info is inconsistent, self-reported, no universal standard.
